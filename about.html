PerformOS is a small software studio based in Brisbane, Australia. We build instruments, not platforms. Four instruments. One studio. No venture funding. No growth targets. No ambition to be everything to everyone.

The operator behind the instruments

Jared Croxton

Jared Croxton leads learning and development and talent acquisition for Accor Plus across seven APAC markets: Australia, New Zealand, India, Indonesia, Philippines, Thailand, and Vietnam. High-volume outbound telesales. Real targets. Real turnover. Real pressure to make training stick and performance measurable.

PerformOS is the studio he built to solve the problems he lives with every day.

How the instruments started

Every instrument came from a real operational problem.

Pocket Customer

Came from watching reps burn leads on live calls. They knew the methodology. They had been through the training. But under pressure, on a live call with a real customer, the script drifted and the deal slipped. No software existed that could coach in real time without a manager listening in. So he built it.

LearnOS

Came from paying too much for a learning management system nobody used. Monthly fees. Per-seat pricing. Content that sat unopened because the platform was too complex and the login was too many clicks away. So he built an LMS that is one purchase, owned forever, and simple enough that uploading a PowerPoint creates a course.

PulseCheck 360

Came from losing good people before anyone noticed they were at risk. Exit interviews revealed what the weekly stand-ups never did: they had been unhappy for months, nobody asked, and by the time the resignation landed it was too late. So he built an instrument that detects flight risk early and gives managers the conversation starters before the conversation is too late.

Performlytics

Came from waiting three days for a report that should have taken 12 seconds. A founder asking "why did revenue spike?" and getting the answer after the board meeting, not before it. So he built an AI operator that reads across the stack and answers in plain English, on demand.
