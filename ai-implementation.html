PerformOS . AI Implementation

AI implementation
for small business.

Most small business AI sits unused. Ours does not. The problem is not the technology. It is the delivery model.

The problem

Small business operators buy AI the same way big enterprises do: monthly subscriptions, per-seat pricing, feature-heavy dashboards, and implementation timelines measured in quarters. Then the software arrives and nobody uses it. The login goes stale. The renewal invoice lands and someone forwards it around asking whether anyone actually logged in last quarter.

PerformOS takes a different approach. We ship instruments, not platforms. We run implementation in four-week sprints, not six-month engagements. And every instrument we build is owned forever after a single purchase.

What AI implementation actually means

One problem. One instrument. One sprint.

For a small business, AI implementation is not about building infrastructure or hiring a data science team. It is about picking one operational problem, choosing the right instrument for it, and getting it running inside your business in under a month. That is the whole job. Everything else is noise.

Better decisions

Numbers sitting in spreadsheets that nobody has time to analyse. Sales figures, customer records, inventory. The instrument: Performlytics.

Better performance

Sales calls that drift off script. Customer conversations that could have gone better but nobody coached them. The instrument: Pocket Customer.

Better visibility

Team members who are burning out or looking elsewhere, and nobody knows until the resignation lands. The instrument: PulseCheck 360.

Better training

An LMS that does not cost $500 a month and still looks empty six months later. The instrument: LearnOS.

The four-week implementation sprint

Fixed scope. Fixed price. No scope creep.

Week one

Scope and configure

We identify the operational problem, map it to the right PerformOS instrument, and configure the instrument to your business context. You upload your data, your sales methodology, your training content, or your team structure. We handle the technical setup.

Week two

Initial deployment

The instrument goes live inside your business. Your first rep runs a roleplay. Your first plain-English question returns an insight. Your first course is built from your content. Your first team check-in goes out.

Week three

Operator onboarding

Your team uses the instrument in real work. We watch the data, adjust the configuration, and answer questions. No training-the-trainer cascade. No change-management programme. Just operators using software that does one job well.

Week four

Handover and ownership

The instrument is yours. You own it. No ongoing fees. No renewal cycle. We hand over the keys and stay available if you need us. Most operators do not. The instrument just works.

Why fixed price matters

No hourly billing. No per-seat pricing.

Consulting firms sell AI implementation by the hour. Software vendors sell it by the seat. Both models reward complexity, because complexity extends the engagement or grows the licence bill.

PerformOS sprints are fixed in scope and fixed in price. You know what you are getting and what it costs before week one begins. If the instrument does not solve your problem, we say so during scoping and we do not start the sprint.

What you do not get

No slide decks. No maturity models. No upsells.

No capability assessments. No stakeholder alignment workshops. No six-month roadmaps. No "phase two" upsells. PerformOS sprints produce one thing: a working AI instrument deployed inside your business. If you want a consulting engagement, there are other studios for that.

Who this is for

This is for operators who

Have a specific operational problem they can name. Are prepared to spend four weeks solving it. Want to own the instrument, not rent it. Would rather ship something that works than wait for something perfect.

This is not for

Businesses that want to "explore AI" without committing to a specific outcome. Businesses that need enterprise procurement and vendor security reviews. Businesses that measure success by the size of the slide deck.
