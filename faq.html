02

AI adoption

Start with one workflow, one tool, and a small policy. PerformOS recommends training the team first, picking one admin or communication workflow to improve, choosing a safe tool, and writing a simple internal policy on what can and cannot go into AI tools.

The order we follow in our adoption roadmaps is: train, pilot, document, scale.

Tools are things a person uses, like ChatGPT or Claude. The person sets the task and reviews the output.

Automations are pre-defined sequences that run without a person, like a Zap that turns a form into a CRM record. They follow rules but do not make new decisions.

Agents are AI systems that make their own decisions about which steps to take, then carry them out. They are more powerful but riskier, so they need clear boundaries and review steps.

Start with high-volume, low-judgement tasks. Common first wins are first-draft emails and proposals, internal knowledge access, customer FAQ responses, meeting notes, and onboarding documents.

Avoid automating anything tied to legal, financial, or compliance decisions before staff are trained. The point of starting small is to build trust in the system and the team.

A first wave of practical adoption usually takes 30 to 90 days. A workshop, a workflow redesign, one or two implementations, and a simple internal policy.

Deeper adoption, including agents, follows once the team is using AI consistently.

03

Training and fluency

AI fluency is the ability of your team to use AI tools safely, confidently, and productively in everyday work. It covers prompting, judgement, safe use, and knowing when to trust or check AI outputs.

For a small business, fluency matters more than tool choice. A team that understands how AI works will get value from almost any tool.

Yes. Most PerformOS workshops are designed for non-technical teams. The goal is practical confidence, not coding.

Staff finish workshops knowing what AI can do for their role, how to use it safely, and what to escalate. We use your actual work as the training material, not hypothetical examples.
